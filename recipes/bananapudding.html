Banana Pudding

Delicious dessert for the family

A delicious dessert for the family can be a warm and comforting homemade apple crisp. With sweet, cinnamon-spiced apples baked beneath a buttery, crunchy oat topping, it’s a perfect combination of flavors and textures. Serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat.

For a lighter but equally satisfying option, consider making a fresh fruit tart. A flaky pastry crust filled with a smooth, creamy custard and topped with a variety of colorful berries offers a delightful balance of sweetness and freshness. It’s a visually stunning dessert that’s both refreshing and elegant, perfect for family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (5 ounce) package instant vanilla pudding mix
  • 3 cups cold skim milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 (8 ounce) container frozen whipped topping, thawed
  • 1/2 (12 ounce) package vanilla wafers
  • 4 bananas, sliced

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Beat cream cheese in a large bowl until fluffy. Beat in condensed milk, then pudding mix. Gradually mix in cold milk until smooth, followed by vanilla.
  3. Fold in 1/2 of the whipped topping.
  4. Line the bottom of a 9x13-inch dish with vanilla wafers.
  5. Arrange sliced bananas evenly on top.
  6. Cover with pudding mixture.
  7. Top with remaining whipped topping.
  8. Cover and chill in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours before serving.
